/ 01 — BACKGROUND

The short version.

I'm a game development student in a 2-year alternance program — alternating between school and professional work. On the company side, I work as the sole Unreal Engine 5 expert on my team. On the school side, I build games with classmates and on my own.

I'm especially drawn to gameplay and UI development, but I also enjoy diving into low-level technical challenges when the opportunity arises. What drives me most is learning — new tools, new engines, new paradigms. If I don't know something, that's just a reason to figure it out.

I'm open to any game company — AAA, indie, or tech — as long as I keep growing technically and making games.

What I care about

values · approach
I like it when the problem is hard, the solution is simple, and I have this "eureka" moment.
▸ 01

Systems over effects.

The fun comes from the rules, not the polish. I try to get the system feeling right first, then decorate.

▸ 02

Understand the layer below.

Writing a Vulkan renderer taught me more about UE5 than UE5 ever did. Going low-level makes the high-level click.

▸ 03

Always learning, on purpose.

New engine, new paradigm, new language — if I keep doing the same thing, I stop getting better. So I keep picking up new things.
